# Eukarya

## Definition
One of three domains of life. Organisms with **eukaryotic cells**: membrane-bound nucleus, organelles, complex internal structure. Includes protists, fungi, plants, and animals.

## Defining Features

### Nuclear Envelope
**Abstraction**: **Separation of information storage and processing**.
- DNA (nucleus) separated from protein synthesis (cytoplasm)
- **Consequence**: Transcription and translation uncoupled (unlike prokaryotes)
- **Regulatory opportunity**: mRNA processing (splicing, capping, polyadenylation)

### Membrane-Bound Organelles
**Abstraction**: **Compartmentalization**.

**Key organelles**:
- **Mitochondria**: ATP production (aerobic respiration)
- **Chloroplasts** (plants, algae): Photosynthesis
- **Endoplasmic reticulum (ER)**: Protein synthesis, lipid synthesis
- **Golgi apparatus**: Protein modification, trafficking
- **Lysosomes**: Degradation, recycling
- **Peroxisomes**: Lipid metabolism, detoxification

**Consequence**: Specialized environments for biochemical reactions. Enables complexity.

### Cytoskeleton
**Abstraction**: **Dynamic internal architecture**.
- **Microtubules**: Structural support, intracellular transport, cell division (spindle)
- **Actin filaments**: Cell shape, motility, cytokinesis
- **Intermediate filaments**: Mechanical strength

**Consequence**: Large cell size (10-100 μm vs. 1-10 μm for prokaryotes), active transport, mitosis/meiosis.

### Linear Chromosomes
- **Multiple chromosomes**: Not single circular (prokaryotes)
- **Histones**: DNA wrapped around histone octamers (nucleosomes)
- **Telomeres**: Chromosome end protection
- **Centromeres**: Kinetochore attachment, chromosome segregation

### Sexual Reproduction (Most)
- **Meiosis**: Reductional division (diploid → haploid)
- **Recombination**: Crossing over, independent assortment
- **Consequence**: Genetic diversity, faster adaptation (vs. asexual)

## Origin: Endosymbiotic Theory
See [[Endosymbiosis]]: **Eukaryotes arose from symbiotic merger**.

### Lynn Margulis (1967)
Proposed mitochondria and chloroplasts were once free-living bacteria, engulfed by archaeal host.

**Evidence**:
- Mitochondria/chloroplasts have own DNA (circular, bacterial-like)
- Own ribosomes (70S, prokaryotic)
- Double membrane (inner = bacterial membrane, outer = host phagosome)
- Phylogeny: Mitochondria from α-proteobacteria, chloroplasts from cyanobacteria

### Chimeric Genome
Eukaryotic genome = **Archaea (host) + Bacteria (organelles)**:
- Nuclear genes: Archaeal origin (information processing)
- Mitochondrial/chloroplast genes: Bacterial origin (energy metabolism)
- Horizontal transfer: Many organellar genes moved to nucleus

## Major Eukaryotic Groups

### Protists (Paraphyletic)
- **Amoebas**: Unicellular, pseudopodia
- **Ciliates**: Paramecium, complex organization
- **Diatoms**: Silica shells, photosynthetic
- **Slime molds**: Social amoebas, multicellular fruiting bodies

### Fungi
- **Decomposers**: Extracellular digestion
- **Chitin cell walls**: Structural polysaccharide
- **Hyphae**: Filamentous growth
- **Examples**: Yeast, mushrooms, molds

### Plants
- **Chloroplasts**: Photosynthesis (endosymbiotic cyanobacteria)
- **Cell walls**: Cellulose
- **Alternation of generations**: Sporophyte/gametophyte
- **Examples**: Mosses, ferns, angiosperms (flowering plants)

### Animals
- **Multicellular heterotrophs**: Ingest food
- **No cell walls**: Flexible ECM (collagen)
- **Nervous system**: Centralized information processing
- **Examples**: Sponges, worms, insects, vertebrates

## Abstraction: Hierarchical Complexity
See [[Hierarchical Composition]]:
- **Prokaryotes**: Molecular → cell (2 levels)
- **Eukaryotes**: Molecular → organelle → cell → (multicellular: tissue → organ → organism) (4-6 levels)

Each level adds emergent properties.

## Size and Complexity Trade-Off
### Prokaryotes
- **Small size**: Diffusion sufficient for transport
- **Simple organization**: No compartments needed
- **Rapid reproduction**: Minutes to hours

### Eukaryotes
- **Large size**: Require active transport (cytoskeleton, motor proteins)
- **Complex organization**: Compartmentalization required
- **Slower reproduction**: Hours to days (or longer)

**Abstraction**: Eukaryotes traded **speed** for **complexity**.

## Multicellularity
See [[Unicellular to Multicellular Transition]]:
- Evolved independently multiple times (animals, plants, fungi, algae)
- Enabled by eukaryotic toolkit (cell adhesion, differentiation, signaling)
- **Consequence**: Complex organisms (trees, whales, humans)
