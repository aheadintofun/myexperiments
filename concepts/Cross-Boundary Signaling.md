# Cross-Boundary Signaling

## Definition
Biological systems communicate across spatial, temporal, and hierarchical boundaries through signaling mechanisms. The recurring pattern: signal → receptor → transduction → response, with noise filtering, amplification, feedback, and crosstalk.

## Universal Signaling Architecture
1. **Signal generation**: Ligand secretion, neurotransmitter release, metabolite diffusion
2. **Transmission**: Across space (paracrine, endocrine) or time (persistent signal)
3. **Reception**: Receptor binding with specificity and affinity
4. **Transduction**: Intracellular cascade amplifies and processes
5. **Response**: Transcriptional, metabolic, behavioral output
6. **Feedback**: Negative (homeostasis) or positive (amplification)

## Examples Across Scales

### Intracellular
Organelle-organelle communication:
- Mitochondrial stress signals to nucleus
- ER-mitochondria calcium transfer
- Lysosomal-mTOR signaling for nutrient sensing

### Intercellular
Cell-cell signaling networks:
- **Cytokines**: Immune cell coordination
- **Growth factors**: Developmental patterning
- **Neurotransmitters**: Synaptic transmission
- **Hormones**: Endocrine coordination across tissues

### Organism-Organism
Inter-organismal communication:
- **Quorum sensing**: Bacterial population coordination
- **Pheromones**: Mating, alarm signals in animals
- **Root exudates**: Plant-microbe signaling in rhizosphere
- **Volatile organic compounds**: Plant-plant communication

### Cross-Kingdom
Host-microbiome signaling:
- Gut microbiome metabolites → host receptors
- Host immune molecules → microbial gene expression
- Symbiotic nitrogen fixation signaling

## Information-Theoretic View
Signaling is **information transmission through noisy channels**:
- Signal-to-noise ratio determines detectability
- Receptor sensitivity sets detection threshold
- Dynamic range of response (linear, ultrasensitive, switch-like)
- Mutual information quantifies channel capacity

## Computational Tools
- **Ligand-receptor databases**: CellPhoneDB, NicheNet, CellChat
- **Pathway analysis**: KEGG, Reactome, WikiPathways
- **Network inference**: SCENIC, GENIE3, ARACNe
- **Spatial signaling**: MISTy, CellChat (spatial mode)

## Key Insight
**Diagnostics are eavesdropping on signaling.** Biomarkers are signals we intercept:
- Tumor markers (PSA, CEA) are secreted signals
- Cytokine panels reveal immune state
- Metabolites in blood/urine are systemic signals
- Imaging captures spatial signaling patterns
