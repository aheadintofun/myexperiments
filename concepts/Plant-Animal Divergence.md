# Plant-Animal Divergence

## Definition
The evolutionary split between **autotrophic, sessile organisms (plants)** and **heterotrophic, mobile organisms (animals)**. Diverged from common eukaryotic ancestor ~1.5 billion years ago.

## What Changed (Abstract)

### Two Fundamentally Different Survival Strategies
**Plants**: **Harvest light, stay put**
**Animals**: **Hunt food, move around**

Every structural difference derives from this strategic divergence.

## Core Distinction: Energy Acquisition

### Plants (Autotrophs)
**Energy source**: Sunlight (photosynthesis)
- $6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{light} \to C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2$
- **Consequence**: Energy abundant (sun everywhere), but low-density
- **Strategy**: Maximize surface area (leaves), roots for nutrients

### Animals (Heterotrophs)
**Energy source**: Consume other organisms
- **Consequence**: Energy concentrated (prey), but must be pursued
- **Strategy**: Locomotion, sensory systems, nervous system

## Structural Consequences

### Cell Walls vs. Flexible Membranes

**Plants**:
- **Cellulose cell wall**: Rigid, structural support
- **Consequence**: Fixed shape, turgor pressure for rigidity
- **Trade-off**: Immobile, can't change shape

**Animals**:
- **No cell wall**: Flexible extracellular matrix (ECM, collagen)
- **Consequence**: Shape-shifting, motility, phagocytosis
- **Trade-off**: Requires internal skeleton (endoskeleton) or external (exoskeleton) for large size

### Sessile vs. Mobile

**Plants**:
- **Sessile**: Rooted in place
- **Advantages**: No energy cost for locomotion, stable access to sunlight
- **Disadvantages**: Can't escape predators, limited mate choice

**Animals**:
- **Mobile**: Locomotion (muscles, flagella, cilia)
- **Advantages**: Pursue prey, escape predators, find mates
- **Disadvantages**: High energy cost

### Information Processing

**Plants**:
- **No nervous system**: Distributed sensing
- **Signaling**: Hormones (auxin, gibberellin, ethylene), electrical signals (action potentials in Venus flytrap)
- **Speed**: Slow (minutes-hours, except specialized cases)

**Animals**:
- **Nervous system**: Centralized information processing
- **Neurons**: Fast electrical signaling (milliseconds)
- **Cephalization**: Brain concentration at anterior end (see [[Cephalization]])
- **Consequence**: Rapid decision-making, complex behavior

### Growth Patterns

**Plants**:
- **Indeterminate growth**: Continue growing throughout life
- **Meristems**: Stem cells at root/shoot tips
- **Modularity**: Branching, repeating units (leaves, flowers)

**Animals**:
- **Determinate growth** (mostly): Fixed adult size
- **Development**: Embryogenesis → larva/juvenile → adult
- **Body plan**: Bilateral symmetry, segmentation

## Developmental Toolkit Divergence

### Plants
- **Hox gene loss**: Plants lost Hox genes (animals retained)
- **MADS-box genes**: Specify flower organ identity (analogous to Hox)
- **Signaling**: Auxin, cytokinin, gibberellin (plant-specific)

### Animals
- **Hox genes**: Specify anterior-posterior axis
- **Signaling**: Wnt, Hedgehog, Notch, TGF-β (shared with other eukaryotes, elaborated in animals)

## Reproductive Strategies

### Plants
- **Alternation of generations**: Sporophyte (diploid) and gametophyte (haploid) stages
- **Pollen**: Airborne sperm (wind/insect pollination)
- **Seeds**: Dormant embryo, dispersal

### Animals
- **Diploid-dominant**: Adults diploid, gametes haploid (brief)
- **Internal fertilization** (many): Direct sperm transfer
- **Parental care** (some): Extended investment in offspring

## Metabolic Differences

### Plants
- **Photorespiration**: Side reaction of Rubisco (wastes energy)
- **C4, CAM photosynthesis**: Adaptations to minimize photorespiration
- **Nitrogen fixation** (legumes): Symbiosis with bacteria

### Animals
- **Urea cycle**: Excrete nitrogen as urea (less toxic than ammonia)
- **Obligate aerobic** (mostly): Require O₂ for respiration
- **Carnivory, herbivory, omnivory**: Diverse diets

## Ecological Roles

### Plants (Primary Producers)
- **Base of food chain**: Convert sunlight → biomass
- **Oxygen production**: Photosynthesis generates O₂
- **Carbon fixation**: Remove CO₂ from atmosphere

### Animals (Consumers)
- **Herbivores**: Consume plants
- **Carnivores**: Consume other animals
- **Decomposers** (with fungi, bacteria): Recycle nutrients

## Convergences Despite Divergence

### Multicellularity
Both independently evolved complex multicellularity (see [[Unicellular to Multicellular Transition]]).

### Sensory Systems
- Plants: Phototropism, gravitropism, thigmotropism
- Animals: Vision, hearing, touch, chemosensation

### Defense Mechanisms
- Plants: Toxins (alkaloids, terpenes), thorns, chemical signaling
- Animals: Venom, armor, camouflage, flight

## Abstract Principles

### Design Constraints
**Plant constraint**: Fixed in place → maximize surface area, distributed sensing
**Animal constraint**: Mobile → streamlined, centralized control

### Energy-Information Trade-Off
- **Plants**: High energy (photosynthesis), low information processing
- **Animals**: Moderate energy (heterotrophy), high information processing (nervous system)
