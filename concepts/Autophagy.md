# Autophagy

## Definition
Cellular self-digestion: the process by which cells **degrade and recycle their own components**. From Greek *auto-* (self) + *phagy* (eating). A fundamental quality control and adaptation mechanism present in all eukaryotes.

## Core Function
Autophagy is controlled catabolism:
- **Degradation**: Breaking down damaged organelles, misfolded proteins, invading pathogens
- **Recycling**: Recovering amino acids, lipids, nucleotides for biosynthesis
- **Quality control**: Removing dysfunctional components
- **Adaptation**: Surviving nutrient deprivation by internal resource mobilization

## Mechanism

### Autophagosome Formation
1. **Initiation**: ULK1 complex activated (mTOR-inhibited, AMPK-activated)
2. **Nucleation**: Beclin-1/VPS34 complex forms isolation membrane
3. **Elongation**: LC3-II lipidation expands membrane (engulfs cargo)
4. **Closure**: Double-membrane autophagosome forms around cargo
5. **Fusion**: Autophagosome fuses with lysosome
6. **Degradation**: Lysosomal enzymes digest contents
7. **Recycling**: Macromolecules broken down, monomers exported to cytoplasm

### Key Molecular Players
- **mTOR**: Master inhibitor (nutrient sensing)
- **AMPK**: Activator (energy stress)
- **Beclin-1**: Autophagosome nucleation
- **LC3**: Membrane expansion marker
- **p62/SQSTM1**: Cargo adaptor (selective autophagy)

## Types of Autophagy

### Macroautophagy
Bulk degradation via autophagosome (described above). The canonical pathway.

### Microautophagy
Direct lysosomal engulfment of cytoplasmic material. No autophagosome formation.

### Chaperone-Mediated Autophagy (CMA)
Selective protein degradation:
- KFERQ motif recognized by HSC70
- Direct translocation into lysosome via LAMP2A
- Highly selective, protein-level precision

### Selective Autophagy
Targeted degradation of specific cargo:
- **Mitophagy**: Damaged mitochondria (PINK1/Parkin pathway)
- **Aggrephagy**: Protein aggregates
- **Pexophagy**: Peroxisomes
- **Xenophagy**: Invading bacteria, viruses
- **ER-phagy**: Endoplasmic reticulum fragments

## Physiological Roles

### Starvation Response
Autophagy provides internal nutrients:
- Muscle: Degrade proteins → amino acids for gluconeogenesis
- Liver: Mobilize glycogen, lipids for energy
- **Survival**: Extends lifespan during famine

### Development
- **Oocyte maturation**: Paternal mitochondria eliminated
- **Metamorphosis**: Tissue remodeling (tadpole tail resorption)
- **Tissue differentiation**: Removing unnecessary organelles

### Quality Control
Continuous housekeeping:
- Remove damaged mitochondria (prevent ROS production)
- Clear misfolded proteins (prevent aggregation)
- Eliminate dysfunctional organelles

### Immunity
- **Xenophagy**: Direct pathogen degradation
- **Antigen presentation**: Autophagy loads MHC class II
- **Inflammasome regulation**: Remove damaged mitochondria (suppress NLRP3)

## Dysregulation and Disease

### Neurodegenerative Diseases
Autophagy impairment → protein aggregation:
- **Alzheimer's**: Amyloid-β, tau accumulation
- **Parkinson's**: α-synuclein aggregates, mitochondrial dysfunction
- **Huntington's**: Polyglutamine aggregates

### Cancer
Autophagy is context-dependent:
- **Tumor suppression**: Removes damaged mitochondria, prevents genomic instability (early stages)
- **Tumor promotion**: Provides nutrients for rapid proliferation (established tumors)

### Aging
Autophagy declines with age:
- Reduced clearance of damaged components
- Accumulation of dysfunctional organelles
- Caloric restriction, rapamycin enhance autophagy → lifespan extension

### Metabolic Disorders
- **Obesity**: Impaired autophagy in adipose tissue, liver
- **Diabetes**: β-cell autophagy required for insulin secretion
- **NAFLD**: Lipophagy defects → lipid accumulation

## Therapeutic Modulation

### Autophagy Inducers
- **Rapamycin**: mTOR inhibitor (immunosuppressant, anti-aging candidate)
- **Metformin**: AMPK activator (diabetes drug, potential longevity agent)
- **Caloric restriction**: Physiological autophagy activator
- **Exercise**: AMPK activation, mitochondrial turnover
- **Spermidine**: Polyamine inducing autophagy, lifespan extension

### Autophagy Inhibitors
- **Chloroquine, hydroxychloroquine**: Block lysosomal acidification (cancer therapy adjuvants)
- **3-methyladenine**: Inhibits VPS34 (research tool)

## Connection to Cachexia
[[Cachexia as Phase Transition]] involves excessive autophagy:
- Systemic inflammation activates autophagy
- Muscle proteolysis exceeds synthesis
- **Therapeutic goal**: Rebalance autophagy-anabolism (see [[Cachexia Inversion]])

[[Selective Catabolism]] can harness autophagy constructively:
- Target dysfunctional components
- Spare functional tissue
- Optimize, don't suppress globally
