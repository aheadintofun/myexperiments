# Immune System as Phenotypic Boundary Hub

## Central Framing

The immune system is the biological instantiation of **boundary enforcement**—the distributed computational system that defines, maintains, and defends the distinction between self and non-self. More than a defense mechanism, it is the arbiter of phenotypic coherence: what belongs to the organism, what should be tolerated, and what must be eliminated.

## The Boundary Problem

Every organism must solve three fundamental challenges:
1. **Recognition**: Distinguish self from non-self at molecular resolution
2. **Tolerance**: Avoid attacking beneficial or necessary entities (self, microbiome, fetus)
3. **Adaptation**: Learn from experience to recognize novel threats

This is not static classification but **dynamic boundary negotiation**—the immune system continuously redefines the phenotypic boundary through cellular computation.

## Computational Perspective

The immune system implements:
- **Pattern recognition**: Receptor repertoires sampling molecular shape space
- **Distributed search**: Millions of clones exploring antigen space in parallel
- **Adaptive optimization**: Somatic hypermutation + selection = gradient descent
- **Context integration**: Same antigen → different response based on costimulation
- **Memory compression**: Expanded clones preserve learned patterns

## Cross-System Themes

### Figure-Ground Decomposition
The immune system IS a figure-ground choice: self = background, pathogens = foreground. Autoimmunity occurs when the frame inverts.

### Symmetry Breaking
Tolerance → immunity represents symmetry breaking. Identical naive cells differentiate into effector vs regulatory lineages.

### Fitness Landscapes
Affinity maturation navigates molecular fitness landscapes through mutation + selection in germinal centers.

### Information Compression
The BCR/TCR repertoire compresses evolutionary experience with pathogens into a finite set of receptor sequences.

### Critical Phenomena
Inflammation exhibits threshold behavior: subcritical (resolution) vs supercritical (chronic).
