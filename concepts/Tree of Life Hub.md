# Tree of Life Hub

## Overview
The phylogenetic tree of life is not merely taxonomy—it is a **record of major organizational transitions**, each representing a **fundamental shift in biological architecture, symmetry breaking, or constraint relaxation**.

This hub focuses on **what changed abstractly** at each transition, not just lineage splitting. What new organizational principle emerged? What constraint was overcome? What symmetry broke?

## Three Domains of Life

### [[Bacteria]]
- Prokaryotic, no nucleus
- Horizontal gene transfer, modular genome
- Metabolic diversity (photosynth, chemosynthesis, fermentation)

### [[Archaea]]
- Prokaryotic but distinct from Bacteria
- Unique membrane chemistry (ether lipids)
- Extremophiles (thermophiles, halophiles, methanogens)

### [[Eukarya]]
- Nuclear envelope, membrane-bound organelles
- Endosymbiotic origin (mitochondria, chloroplasts)
- Complex multicellularity enabled

## Major Transitions (Abstract Principles)

### [[Origin of Life]]
**What changed**: Non-living → living, chemistry → biology
- **Abiogenesis**: From prebiotic chemistry to self-replicating systems
- **RNA World Hypothesis**: RNA as both information carrier and catalyst
- **Metabolism-first vs. replication-first**: Which came first?

### [[Prokaryote to Eukaryote Transition]]
**What changed**: Compartmentalization, endosymbiosis, scale-up
- **Endosymbiosis**: Captured bacteria become organelles (Lynn Margulis)
- **Nuclear envelope**: Separates transcription/translation (regulatory opportunity)
- **Cytoskeleton**: Intracellular transport, dynamic structure
- **Larger size**: 10-100x volume increase

### [[Unicellular to Multicellular Transition]]
**What changed**: Individual → collective, competition → cooperation
- **Cell adhesion**: Cells remain together post-division
- **Differentiation**: Same genome, different cell fates
- **Division of labor**: Specialized cell types (soma, germline)
- **Cheater suppression**: Evolutionary mechanisms enforce cooperation

### [[Plant-Animal Divergence]]
**What changed**: Autotrophy vs. heterotrophy, sessile vs. mobile strategies
- **Energy acquisition**: Photosynthesis (plants) vs. ingestion (animals)
- **Structural strategy**: Cell walls, rigidity (plants) vs. ECM, flexibility (animals)
- **Motility**: Sessile (plants, mostly) vs. mobile (animals)
- **Nervous system**: Animals evolved centralized information processing

### [[Vertebrate Innovations]]
**What changed**: Neural crest, endoskeleton, adaptive immunity
- **Neural crest**: Embryonic cell population enabling craniofacial complexity, peripheral nervous system
- **Endoskeleton**: Internal support (vs. exoskeleton), enables larger size
- **Adaptive immunity**: V(D)J recombination, somatic hypermutation
- **Cephalization**: Brain centralization, sensory organs concentrated anteriorly

### [[Mammalian Innovations]]
**What changed**: Endothermy, live birth, parental investment, neocortex
- **Endothermy**: Metabolic heat generation, homeothermy
- **Placenta**: Direct maternal-fetal nutrient exchange (live birth in most)
- **Lactation**: Milk production, extended parental care
- **Neocortex expansion**: Cognitive capacity, behavioral flexibility

## Abstract Organizing Principles

### [[Endosymbiosis]]
**Principle**: Symbiotic merger creates new composite organism
- Mitochondria, chloroplasts as captured bacteria
- Lynn Margulis (1967): Endosymbiotic theory
- **Result**: Massive metabolic scaling (aerobic respiration, photosynthesis)

### [[Multicellularity as Cooperation]]
**Principle**: Cells cooperate instead of competing
- Evolutionary game theory: Cooperation stable if benefits exceed costs
- **Cheater suppression**: Mechanisms preventing rogue cells (cancer-like)
- **Examples**: Germline sequestration, apoptosis, immune surveillance

### [[Developmental Toolkit]]
**Principle**: Conserved gene regulatory modules reused across contexts
- **Hox genes**: Specify body axis patterning (flies to humans)
- **Signaling pathways**: Wnt, Hedgehog, Notch, TGF-β superfamily
- **Modularity**: Same toolkit, different wiring → morphological diversity

### [[Cephalization]]
**Principle**: Concentration of nervous system and sensory organs at anterior end
- Bilateral symmetry → anterior-posterior axis
- **Advantages**: Directed locomotion, faster sensory processing
- **Result**: Brain evolution, head formation

## Hierarchical Composition
See [[Hierarchical Composition]]: Each transition adds a new organizational level.

```
Prebiotic molecules
└─> Cells (Origin of Life)
    └─> Eukaryotic cells (Endosymbiosis)
        └─> Multicellular organisms (Cooperation)
            └─> Complex organisms (Specialized tissues/organs)
                └─> Social groups (Eusociality, human societies)
```

At each level, new **emergent properties** arise.

## Symmetry Breaking Events
See [[Symmetry Breaking in Biological Systems]]:
- **Origin of Life**: Homochirality (L-amino acids, D-sugars)
- **Eukaryogenesis**: Compartmentalization breaks spatial symmetry
- **Multicellularity**: Soma-germline distinction breaks reproductive symmetry
- **Bilateral symmetry**: Left-right, dorsal-ventral, anterior-posterior axes
- **Cephalization**: Breaks anterior-posterior symmetry (head ≠ tail)

## Evolutionary Constraints and Releases

### Constraints Imposed
- **Genetic code**: Frozen accident, nearly universal
- **DNA/RNA/protein**: Central dogma, information flow direction
- **Metabolic pathways**: Glycolysis, TCA cycle highly conserved

### Constraints Released
- **Prokaryote → Eukaryote**: Size limit removed (intracellular transport via cytoskeleton)
- **Unicellular → Multicellular**: Complexity limit removed (differentiation enables specialization)
- **Aquatic → Terrestrial**: Desiccation, gravity constraints (amniotic egg, seeds)
